Help & overview
For PMs & admins
What is Arbiter? ▾

Arbiter is a scorecard for incoming feature and project requests. Reps submit through the rep portal; you and other PMs score, decide, and the rep gets notified.

Every request is evaluated against the same weighted criteria — revenue potential, strategic alignment, effort, and so on — producing a 0–100 score and a tier (pursue, evaluate, defer, pass). The decision is yours; the score gives the conversation a shared starting point.

Dashboard ▾

Your home base. Every saved project lives here in either List or Board view. The summary cards at the top — Total, This month, Avg score, Acceptance rate, Overdue revisits — give you a quick read on the pipeline.

Filter by status chip, type, tier, date, or rep. Search by project or customer name. Click a project to expand its details inline; from there you can change status, add decision notes, or set a revisit date.

Switch to Board to see projects laid out as a kanban grouped by status. Drag a card to a new column to change its status — the change persists.

Status & decision workflow ▾

A request moves through statuses as the team works it: Submitted → Under Review → Accepted, Deferred, or Passed. Completed is the terminal state for accepted projects that have shipped.

When you change a project's status — or add decision notes, or set a revisit date — and click Save, Arbiter automatically emails the original submitter. They see the new status, your notes, and any revisit date. They never see the score or tier (those are internal).

Deferred projects benefit from a revisit date. The Dashboard's Overdue revisits card surfaces ones whose date has passed, so nothing slips out of mind.

New request tab ▾

For times when you need to score a request yourself rather than wait for a rep to submit. Open the + button in the bottom-right of the Dashboard.

Pick a project type, fill in the locked fields (project name, customer, submitter, etc.) and any custom detail fields, then answer each scoring criterion. The score and tier update live as you answer. Click Save to add it to the Dashboard.

Notifications ▾

Arbiter sends transactional emails for two events: new submissions (sent to all workspace members) and project updates (sent to the rep when status, decision notes, or revisit date change).

In Settings → Workspace members, each member has an Email me on new submissions checkbox on their own row. Uncheck it to opt out of those notifications. Project updates always go to the rep — they're decisions the rep needs to know about.

Settings → Recent notifications shows the last 50 send attempts with status pills (Sent, Failed, Skipped). Hover a Failed pill to see the error.

Workspace members ▾

Admins and PMs who have access to this workspace. Manage them in Settings → Workspace members.

To add someone, enter their email and choose a role. The user must already have a confirmed Arbiter account — you'll see an inline error if they don't. Remove a member by clicking Remove; you can't remove or demote yourself (another admin has to do that for you).

Today admin and PM roles have the same permissions. The labels are for your own organization.

Reps tab ▾

The list of authorized submitters for this workspace. Reps are people who can sign into the rep portal and submit requests; they can't see scoring, decisions, or anyone else's projects.

Add a rep with their name and email. Use Copy portal link on a rep's card to generate a per-rep URL that pre-fills their email at sign-in. Use Deactivate to revoke access without losing their submission history.

The filter chips group reps by status, and clicking a rep card filters the Dashboard to just their projects.

The rep portal ▾

Reps sign in at /arbiter/portal/ with a magic link. They see only their own submissions, can submit new requests, and edit a submission while it's still in Submitted status.

Reps never see scores, tiers, weights, or other reps' projects. Their dashboard shows status, decision notes, and revisit dates — the same things they get in their notification emails.

If you want to preview what a rep sees, sign out of the main app, open the portal URL, and sign in as a rep account.

Edit criteria tab ▾

Define the questions reps see when submitting and the answer options each criterion offers. Open from the gear icon in the top-right.

Each criterion has a label, an optional hint, and a list of options — each option has a label and a 1–10 score. You can also add custom detail fields (text, select, date) that aren't scored but capture context.

Existing projects keep their original criteria via a snapshot — changes here only affect new submissions, so historical scores stay comparable.

Scoring weights tab ▾

Tune how much each criterion contributes to the final score. Total weight across criteria normalizes to 100%, and the tier thresholds at the bottom decide how a numeric score maps to Pursue, Evaluate, Defer, or Pass.

Project types let you apply different weight profiles for different request kinds — a feature ask might weight strategic alignment heavily, while a bug fix might weight revenue impact lower.

Use the Presets bar to start from a sensible default and adjust from there.

Insights tab ▾

Aggregate analytics across all projects in the workspace. Submissions over time, status breakdown, tier distribution, request volume by type.

Useful for retrospectives, status updates to leadership, or noticing patterns — like a sudden spike in deferred requests from one segment.

Backing up settings ▾

Found in Settings → Settings backup. Exports your scoring rubric — criteria, weights, presets, project types, tier thresholds — as a JSON file.

Use it to back up before experimenting with the configurator, or to copy a rubric to another workspace. Import to restore.

Projects are backed up separately in Settings → Projects backup; that one preserves all scoring data and snapshots so a restore is faithful.
